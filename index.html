About EPIC PATHSHALA

Biswajit Jha, affectionately known as "Book Dada," is a social entrepreneur and educator dedicated to uplifting underprivileged children in North Bengal. After a successful career as a sports editor at Zee News in Delhi, he and his wife, Dr. Sanjukta Saha, left their high-profile jobs to return to their roots in Mendabari, a tribal village in West Bengal. There, they founded the Ranjganj Maa Sarada Shishu Tirtha Charitable Educational Society, which runs a free school and football academy for tribal children.

Jha's initiatives include establishing free coaching centers and providing educational support to children in Mendabari and surrounding areas. He employs local teachers and receives support from donors to sustain these efforts. His work has impacted over a thousand children, offering them opportunities for education and personal growth.

Through his dedication, Jha has become a beacon of hope for many, demonstrating that one individual's commitment can lead to transformative change in a community. Learn More About Biswajit Jha

Education

Biswajit Jha, known as "Book Dada," is a social entrepreneur dedicated to uplifting underprivileged children in North Bengal. He co-founded Epic Public School in Cooch Behar in 2013, focusing on inclusive education, and later initiated the Mandabari Model Tribal Village project in Alipurduar in 2021. These initiatives offer free coaching, computer labs, and libraries to tribal children, aiming to bridge educational gaps and create self-employment opportunities.

Football Academy

Biswajit Jha's football academy in Mendabari provides tribal children with opportunities to develop athletic skills while continuing their education. The academy has produced several talented players who have competed at district and state levels. Combining sports with academics, the program teaches discipline, teamwork, and perseverance, offering a pathway to potential sports scholarships and careers.
